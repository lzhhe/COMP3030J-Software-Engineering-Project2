working flow

The steps we will work

All waste will be handled according to the following procedures. Thank you for your contribution to protecting the environment

Unconfirmed

  • Orders for waste disposal are submitted with complete attribute information, such as type, quantity, and source of the waste.

  • These details are crucial for determining the treatment method and estimating costs.

  • After submission, orders await review by administrators to ensure all necessary information is provided and complies with the standards and regulations of the waste management center.

  • Administrators may contact the submitter for additional details or clarification if needed.

  • This stage is the starting point of the process, ensuring that all subsequent steps can proceed smoothly.

Confirmed

  • Once an order is confirmed by administrators, it indicates acceptance and readiness to begin processing the waste.

  • Waste materials are collected and transported according to a predetermined schedule.

  • During this stage, waste may be temporarily stored before further processing.

  • Transport and storage must adhere to strict safety and environmental protection standards to prevent pollution.

  • Simultaneously, the waste management center may start preparing resources and equipment needed for processing, such as scheduling personnel and equipment.

Processing

  • Upon arrival at the treatment facility, waste materials begin specialized, targeted harmless treatment.

  • Treatment methods are selected based on the type and characteristics of the waste, which may include physical, chemical, or biological technologies.

  • The goal of this stage is to reduce or eliminate the hazardous nature of the waste, transforming it into a form that is harmless or poses minimal harm to the environment.

  • Treatment must strictly follow environmental protection regulations and safety operation procedures to prevent secondary pollution.

  • By-products such as energy recovery and metal recycling may be generated during treatment, which can be used for further resource utilization.

Finished

  • Once the waste materials have undergone harmless treatment, the process moves to the finished stage.

  • This signifies that the waste is no longer hazardous and can be safely disposed of or utilized.

  • Treated waste may be sent to landfills for final disposal or resourced based on its characteristics.

  • For example, metals and recyclable materials may be recovered and reused, reducing the demand for new resources.

  • The waste management center records and reports the treatment outcomes to demonstrate that the waste has been properly treated according to regulations.

  • The completion of this stage marks the end of an order and provides valuable data and experience for the waste management center to improve future processes.
